## 1. Data Analysis

- **Rows**: 30,000

- **Target**: `eligible` (binary: `Yes`/`No`)
- **Features**: `age`, `gender`, `category`, `state`, `occupation`, `annual_income`, `disability`, `is_minority`

The dataset is moderately imbalanced toward **Not Eligible**. We use a stratified 80/20 split to preserve the class ratio across train and test.

## 7. Conclusion
- **Performance**: Random Forest performs better overall (higher Recall, F1, and AUC), which is important for identifying eligible citizens.
- **Interpretability**: Decision Tree is easier to explain and audit, which is crucial in government workflows.
- **Recommendation**: Use **Random Forest** for production scoring when performance is the priority, but keep a **Decision Tree** model for explainability in audits and policy review.
- **If interpretability is critical** (e.g., citizen appeals or legal review), Decision Tree may be preferred despite its slightly lower performance.
